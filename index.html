Project Deep Dive

MATH 112 Redesign

Hero Image: The New Visual Homepage & Linear Module Path

The Solution

Role

Instructional Design Specialist

Tools

Canvas, Articulate Rise

Timeline

3 Months

Outcome

Double-digit Pass Rate ↑

The Challenge

Historically high attrition rates signaled a disconnect between student effort and course outcomes.

The Fix

  • Reduced Cognitive Load
  • Constructive Alignment
  • Weekly Scaffolding

Diagnosis: Cognitive Overload

An instructional audit revealed two root causes for the failure rate: confusing LMS navigation that forced students to "hunt" for content, and assessments that didn't align with learning objectives. My objective was to transform the course into a learner-centered environment.

Action: Structural & Pedagogical Redesign

Structural Redesign: I reorganized the content into consistent weekly modules and built a visual homepage to create a clear, linear learning path.

Pedagogical Alignment: I restructured the assessment strategy to ensure every high-stakes exam was directly supported by measurable objectives and preparatory practice modules.

The Result

We saw a double-digit percentage increase in pass rates and a marked reduction in course withdrawals year-over-year.

About Me

With a Ph.D. in Instructional Technology and extensive experience in higher education, I bridge the gap between complex pedagogy and intuitive digital experiences.

I believe that learning is a universal language. My background in Linguistics and TESOL (University of Edinburgh) informs my approach to accessibility—I see every course design challenge as a translation problem: How do we translate expert knowledge into a language students can understand?

Since 2017, I have served as an Instructional Design Specialist and Manager at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where I lead initiatives to modernize curriculum, mentor junior designers, and implement data-driven strategies that measurably improve student retention.

Data-Driven Strategy

Design decisions shouldn't be guesses. I use LMS analytics and engagement metrics to iterate and improve course architecture.

Inclusivity First (UDL)

Accessibility is not an afterthought. I build with Universal Design for Learning principles to ensure equity for all learners.

Faculty Partnership
